Gender, Ambiguity, and Queer Expression in The Smiths' Music

The Disruption of Normativity: Queer Desire and Negativity in Morrissey and The Smiths

Overview

The final section, “Gender, Ambiguity, and Queer Expression in The Smiths' Music” is an argument that incorporates insights from Fredrick Rukes' The Disruption of Normativity: Queer Desire and Negativity in Morrissey and The Smiths. I examine how Morrissey and the band as a whole address queer desire and ambiguity within their lyrics. Rukes argues that The Smiths reject traditional narratives and critique societal norms, with Morrissey recognizing sexual and gender fluidity in his lyrics. By challenging conventional ideas of gender and sexuality, Morrissey disrupts standard social systems. This section particularly analyzes songs from The Queen Is Dead, which aligns with my focus on how Morrissey’s lyrics reflect his challenge to conventional understandings of gender and sexuality. (Rukes, 2017).
